Cookie Policy

Last Updated: November 10, 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how Oxford Response uses cookies and similar technologies on our website. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences, improve functionality, and provide analytical data about site usage.

Cookies can be:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent Cookies: Cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until manually deleted
  • First-Party Cookies: Set by Oxford Response
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by external services we use

2. How We Use Cookies

Oxford Response uses cookies for the following purposes:

2.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for our website to function properly:

  • Maintaining your session and login status
  • Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Ensuring website security
  • Load balancing and performance optimization

These cookies cannot be disabled as they are essential for the website to work.

2.2 Analytics Cookies

We use analytics cookies to understand how visitors interact with our website:

  • Number of visitors and page views
  • Pages visited and time spent on site
  • Traffic sources and navigation patterns
  • Device types and browser information

This data is anonymized and helps us improve our website experience.

2.3 Functionality Cookies

These cookies enhance your experience by remembering:

  • Your language preferences
  • Display settings and customizations
  • Previously entered information
  • Content you've viewed or interacted with

2.4 Performance Cookies

Performance cookies help us monitor and improve website performance:

  • Page load times and responsiveness
  • Error detection and debugging
  • Server performance metrics
  • User experience optimization

3. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set their own cookies:

3.1 Google Analytics

We may use Google Analytics to analyze website traffic. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect anonymous data about:

  • Visitor demographics and interests
  • Site usage and behavior patterns
  • Conversion tracking and goals

Learn more: Google Privacy Policy

3.2 Social Media

Social media widgets (Discord, YouTube, etc.) may set cookies when embedded on our site to:

  • Enable social sharing features
  • Track interactions with social content
  • Personalize social media experiences

3.3 Content Delivery Networks (CDN)

We use CDNs to deliver content efficiently. These services may use cookies for:

  • Performance optimization
  • Security and DDoS protection
  • Geographic load balancing

5. Managing Cookies

You have several options to manage or disable cookies:

5.1 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete cookies
  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Set preferences for specific websites

Instructions for popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Microsoft Edge
  • Safari

5.2 Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of specific tracking:

  • Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Digital Advertising Alliance Opt-out

5.3 Impact of Disabling Cookies

Please note: Disabling cookies may affect website functionality:

  • Some features may not work properly
  • Preferences may not be saved
  • User experience may be degraded
  • You may need to re-enter information

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers support "Do Not Track" (DNT) signals. Currently, there is no industry standard for responding to DNT signals. We do not alter our data collection practices in response to DNT signals at this time.

7. Mobile Devices

Mobile devices may use identifiers similar to cookies. You can manage these through your device settings:

  • iOS: Settings > Privacy > Advertising > Limit Ad Tracking
  • Android: Settings > Google > Ads > Opt out of Ads Personalization

8. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect changes in:

  • Technologies we use
  • Legal requirements
  • Our business practices

Check this page regularly for updates. The "Last Updated" date indicates when the policy was last revised.

For questions about our use of cookies, contact us through:
